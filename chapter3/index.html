    Main idea

    The charter sets direction. The process turns direction into action.

    Project charter

    Purpose, goals, audience, success indicators, strategies, and tactics.

    Implementation plan

    People, content, technology, schedule, budget, approvals, testing, and maintenance.

    Process protects the project from vague decisions, late content, unclear roles, and accidental scope creep.

    Project resources

    A website needs more than one kind of skill.

    01Strategy

    Purpose, goals, priorities, and value.

    02Project management

    Timeline, budget, scope, approvals, and coordination.

    03UX + information architecture

    Research, content organization, user tasks, and usability.

    04Design

    Visual direction, layout, interaction, and consistency.

    05Technology

    HTML/CSS/JS, CMS, hosting, data, security, and performance.

    06Production

    Pages, content entry, media, QA, publishing, and updates.

    On small projects, one person may wear several hats. The responsibilities still exist.

    Collaboration

    “Throw it over the wall” creates avoidable problems.

    Disconnected workflow

    • Strategy finishes, then disappears
    • Design happens without content
    • Developers receive late surprises
    • Testing happens after decisions feel final

    Cross-disciplinary workflow

    • Research, content, design, and tech talk early
    • Risks surface before build
    • Design uses real content and constraints
    • Testing improves the project while changes are still possible

    Better process does not remove conflict. It makes conflict visible early enough to solve.

    Content development

    The site cannot be finished if the content is imaginary.

    Real content exposes real problems: missing pages, unclear labels, weak copy, image needs, legal issues, and maintenance responsibilities.

    The lorem ipsum trap

    A layout can look “done” with fake text. The project is not done until real content fits the structure, voice, and user task.

    Content production should begin early and continue through testing and launch.

    Budget and timeline

    Small hidden costs can become large project risks.

    High risk The project may look cheaper than it really is because important work is missing from the plan.
    AccessibilityCan people with different abilities use it?
    SecurityWhat data or systems need protection?
    SupportWho fixes problems after launch?
    MaintenanceWho keeps content fresh and accurate?

    Site construction

    Building pages is one of the last major stages, not the first.

    1

    Architecture

    The pages, labels, and navigation structure are understood.

    2

    Content

    Text, images, media, and data needs are known.

    3

    Design system

    Templates, layout patterns, and interface behavior are ready to apply.

    Rushing into page construction creates rework. Good process turns building into assembly, testing, and refinement.
