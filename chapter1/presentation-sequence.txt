WST 157 · Chapter 1 Presentation Sequence

1. Opening
Start with the question: What makes a website successful?
Expected answers may include appearance, speed, easy navigation, useful information, trust, and completing an action.

2. Strategy
Use the contrast between “make it look modern” and a measurable website goal.
Main point: design choices are stronger when the project has a clear reason to exist.

3. Diagnose · Guide · Act
Connect the three steps to real projects: understand the situation, create decision rules, and choose actions.

4. Website Purpose Activity
Generate a client. For the selected client, identify purpose, audience, action, and success.

5. Client Wants vs. User Needs
Compare the two columns. Discuss how good planning balances both sides.

6. Priority Picker
Select three first-version priorities. Discuss how feature choices affect scope and usefulness.

7. Scope
Move the slider. Discuss how too little scope can be weak and too much scope can become unmanageable.

8. Site Types
Click different site types. Notice how planning questions change depending on the type of website.

9. Roles
Discuss who approves, creates, maintains, and resolves conflicts in a website project.

10. Road Map
Walk through the phases from strategy to testing and improvement.

11. Project Charter
Introduce the six sections: purpose, goals, audience, success indicators, strategies, and tactics.

12. Example Charter
Use the mobile car wash example to show the difference between goals, strategies, and tactics.

13. Charter Studio
Draft a simple charter. Short phrases are enough for the first version.

14. Checkpoint
Use the review questions to reinforce purpose, priorities, and tactics.

15. Exit Ticket
Complete the sentence: Before designing a website, the most important thing to clarify is ________ because ________.
